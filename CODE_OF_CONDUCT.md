# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as members, contributors, and leaders, pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience
for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race,
religion, or sexual orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward others.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Accepting responsibility for mistakes, apologizing to those impacted, and learning from the experience.
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals but for the overall community.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments, as well as personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information—such as physical or email addresses—without explicit permission.
- Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Community Engagement

To support a thriving and welcoming community, we encourage contributors to:

- Welcome and assist newcomers patiently.
- Strive to resolve non-critical misunderstandings directly and respectfully.
- Lead by example and model excellent conduct within collaborative spaces.
- Offer constructive feedback with actionable solutions when appropriate.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take
appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior deemed inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or
harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits,
issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct. Whenever appropriate, leaders will
communicate reasons for moderation decisions and ensure transparency in their actions.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces associated with the project, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories
- Communication platforms (e.g., Discord, Slack, mailing lists)
- Social media accounts
- Online or offline events where members represent the community

Representation of the community in public spaces (e.g., using an official email or social media account) also requires
adhering to this Code of Conduct.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible
for enforcement at **[your-reporting-email@domain.com]**. Anonymous reports will also be considered, though they may
limit the ability to investigate fully.

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Community leaders are obligated to respect the
privacy and security of the reporter and all parties involved.

Failure of leadership to take fair and appropriate measures may result in the reassessment of their leadership
responsibilities.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will adhere to the following impact guidelines when determining the consequences of violations:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.  
**Consequence**: A private, written warning with an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A request for a
public apology may be included.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or a series of actions.  
**Consequence**: A written warning and temporary restrictions on interactions, including refraining from direct
communication with affected individuals. Noncompliance may lead to escalation.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of standards or sustained inappropriate behavior.  
**Consequence**: A temporary ban from the community and all forms of interaction. Further violations during this period
may result in a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Severe or repeated violations, including patterns of harassment or sustained aggression.  
**Consequence**: A permanent ban from all community participation.
